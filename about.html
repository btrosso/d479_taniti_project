About the Island

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Taniti, a hidden gem in the Pacific, boasts an enchanting blend of natural beauty and cultural richness. Spanning less than 500 square miles, this small tropical island offers a diverse landscape that includes pristine sandy beaches and rugged rocky shores, perfect for explorers and sunbathers alike. Nestled within its varied terrain is a small yet safe harbor, surrounded by lush, tropical rainforests that beckon nature enthusiasts. The island's mountainous interior features a stunning, active volcano that adds a touch of adventure to its serene environment. Home to an indigenous population of about 20,000, Taniti has recently experienced a surge in tourism, transitioning from an economy traditionally rooted in fishing and agriculture to one that warmly welcomes visitors eager to discover its unique charm.

Taniti offers a vibrant array of amenities to ensure a memorable stay for its visitors. The island boasts ten diverse restaurants, including local seafood spots, American-style diners, and Pan-Asian eateries. Grocery needs are met by two supermarkets, two smaller grocery stores, and a 24-hour convenience store. Lodging options range from budget-friendly hostels to a luxurious four-star resort, with many charming family-owned hotels and bed and breakfasts. Entertainment abounds with beach activities, rainforest exploration, and volcano visits, complemented by museums, chartered fishing tours, snorkeling, zip-lining, pubs, a microbrewery, a dance club, movies, helicopter rides, arcades, art galleries, and bowling. Sightseeing highlights include the native architecture and white sandy beaches of Taniti City, along with island tours and hikes. Convenient transportation options include a small airport, public and private buses, taxis, rental cars, and bicycles. Essential information for visitors includes 120-volt power outlets, alcohol regulations, language proficiency, healthcare facilities, and currency usage. Taniti's blend of natural beauty and modern conveniences makes it an ideal destination for travelers seeking both adventure and relaxation.
